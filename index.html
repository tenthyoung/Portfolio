About Me

I have spent about three years in teaching, finance, marketing, and customer service. There was one common theme that I found in all of my work experience, things were inefficient. And I always thought of ways things could improve. For example, my math students could have improved much more dramatically had they had the right software to combine fun with spaced repetition and learning. The finance firm had so many repetitive tasks that could easily be automated. The start up I worked at failed entirely because of inefficiencies in their code.

Again and again, wherever I looked I felt like things could be so easily improved with better software, but I didn't have the abilities yet to build any of it. It was from that point on that I started to code more on my free time. This finally brought me to attend a coding bootcamp at UCSD, where I have finally developed a well-rounded understanding of web development to aid my Udemy courses.

As cheesy as it sounds, I truly believe that coding is very much in its own way a form of modern wizardry as Elon Musk describes. We essentially can become like modern day alchemists and create something out of nothing. In an instant, we can deploy solutions and value to billions of people around the world. I am not sure I can or will ever find another field where I could do something like this.
